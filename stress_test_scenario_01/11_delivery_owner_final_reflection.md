# Delivery Owner Final Reflection

Status: working draft  
Perspective: Delivery Owner / Project Manager

## 1. Overall judgment

### Was the framework usable in practice for this request?

Yes, with reservations.

It was usable enough to stop the Delivery Owner from guessing, to start at Stage 1, and to justify a scaled path instead of a full initiative pack. It was not usable in a purely mechanical way. Judgment was still needed to decide what "small but governed" means when ownership is ambiguous.

### What parts helped most?

- the instruction to start at Stage 1 when the current stage is unclear
- the distinction between small governed work and a fuller initiative path
- the [Work Brief](../work_delivery/work_brief/work_brief_specification.md) as a lighter control record
- the repeated insistence on naming owners, decision authority, and acceptance basis
- the reminder that work should not quietly start because it feels urgent

### What parts were hardest to apply?

- deciding how much Stage 1 assessment is enough for a request this small
- working out how to handle split authority between office usefulness and official-record correctness
- deciding whether leave and itinerary status belong in one work item without forcing a larger scope than the requestor intended
- knowing when the Work Brief is enough for the technical team and when a separate support note is also needed

## 2. Assumptions and friction

### What assumptions had to be made?

- that Angie would act as sponsor and delegated decision authority for a small controlled item
- that the office operations owner is the likely outcome owner, even though the exact person is still a placeholder
- that leave records already exist and are separately owned
- that the first useful release can be a reporting view rather than a workflow engine
- that Microsoft 365 is the available and proportionate platform direction

### What created delivery friction?

- no explicit sponsor at intake
- no explicit outcome owner at intake
- no explicit acceptance authority at intake
- no clear answer on whether usefulness acceptance and record-correctness acceptance are the same thing
- tension between Jane's desire for simplicity and the Committee Secretary's need for formal correctness
- the framework does not itself resolve cross-record source-of-truth conflicts

### What reduced ambiguity?

- Angie pushing for proportionate control instead of overbuild
- the Committee Secretary insisting that the approved itinerary record remains controlling
- the Programmer forcing a system-of-record decision before build
- the Work Brief path, which gave the Delivery Owner a place to keep scope, owners, risks, and decisions visible without escalating to a full initiative definition

## 3. Weight, vagueness, and rollout

### What felt too heavy?

- running three Stage 1 artifacts for a request that feels small can feel heavy unless the Delivery Owner keeps them concise
- the framework is rich enough that practitioners could over-document small work if they are not disciplined

### What felt too vague?

- the exact threshold for when a Work Brief is sufficient versus when a fuller initiative record is needed
- how to handle split acceptance ownership in practice
- how much supporting definition detail is acceptable before Stage 3 without drifting into Stage 4 behavior

### What should change before wider rollout?

- add a sharper small-work decision aid with explicit examples
- add guidance for split acceptance models, especially where data correctness and business usefulness belong to different roles
- add clearer guidance on source-of-truth conflicts when a solution combines multiple official record sets

### Would the Delivery Owner use this framework again for similar work?

Yes, but only if it is applied pragmatically.

I would use it again because it prevented a sloppy jump from request to build and forced the right questions into view. I would not use it as a rigid checklist for every small office request, because that would likely create resistance faster than it creates clarity.

## 4. Final candid answer

The framework works best as a navigation spine, not as a substitute for judgment.

For this request, it helped the Delivery Owner:

- slow down just enough
- choose a proportionate path
- expose missing ownership
- protect the work from a naive "just build a tracker" response

It struggled when the Delivery Owner needed exact operational guidance for a small item with mixed record authority. That is where the framework was still interpretive and where a less experienced Delivery Owner would probably hesitate or improvise.
