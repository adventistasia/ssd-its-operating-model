# Conversations And Decisions

Status: working draft
Purpose: capture the conversations that changed the Delivery Owner's view in real time.

## 1. Conversation 1 - Jane and the request shape

### What Jane said

- staff need one place to check availability
- the current approach is too fragmented
- she does not want a tool that creates more admin work than it removes

### What the Delivery Owner took from it

- the need is real and repetitive
- the solution must be low-maintenance
- user simplicity matters, but it cannot override record authority

## 2. Conversation 2 - Angie and the sponsorship question

### What Angie said

- she supports the problem being solved
- she does not want a duplicate system of record
- she wants the work to stay useful, practical, and not bureaucratic

### What the Delivery Owner took from it

- Angie is a plausible sponsor
- the work now has a real business sponsor who can make decisions
- sponsorship depends on keeping the solution bounded to visibility and coordination

## 3. Conversation 3 - Programmer and the hidden complexity

### What the Programmer said

- the tracker cannot guess status from informal notes
- itinerary revisions need a visible history
- HR leave data cannot be casually copied into another owned list
- a quick build would hard-code the wrong process if the rules are not settled

### What the Delivery Owner took from it

- the request is not just a list or form
- source-of-truth decisions must come before build thinking
- the work is still small enough to manage, but not small enough for a casual Work Brief

## 4. Conversation 4 - Committee Secretary and HR ownership

### What the Committee Secretary said

- itinerary approval is formal
- latest approved itinerary status must stay traceable
- informal summaries must not replace the approved record

### What the HR Leave Records Owner said

- leave status is controlled HR data
- visibility can be allowed, but ownership must stay with HR
- leave data should not be duplicated without a clear purpose and control basis

### What the Delivery Owner took from it

- the solution must respect multiple authoritative owners
- split acceptance is real and must remain visible
- the work needs a fuller baseline than a Work Brief can safely hold

## 5. Decisions made during the simulation

| Decision | Outcome | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Start at Stage 1 | Yes | The stage was not obvious at intake |
| Continue past initial review | Yes | The problem is real and recurring |
| Escalate beyond Work Brief | Yes | Multiple owners, access concerns, and revision logic make the work broader |
| Use an Initiative Definition Document | Yes | The work needs a controlled baseline that can carry split ownership and acceptance |
| Stop before execution | Yes | The exercise is only a readiness simulation, not a build or deployment simulation |

## 6. Candid framework friction

- the framework is good at forcing ownership questions into the open
- it is less helpful when the request is small but the control surface is broad
- the Delivery Owner still has to judge how much detail is enough without turning Stage 2 into Stage 4
- split acceptance is possible, but the framework makes the Delivery Owner explain it clearly rather than hide it
