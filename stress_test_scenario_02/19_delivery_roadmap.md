## 3. Dependencies and assumptions

- HR will confirm leave status access and privacy boundaries
- Committee Secretary will confirm itinerary revision visibility
- IT Service Owner will confirm the support path
- the same employee identifier will work across the controlled sources
- the first release remains a visibility layer, not a workflow replacement

## 4. Scope and deliverable alignment

- Stage 2 deliverables define the controlled baseline
- Stage 3 authorizes the work with conditions
- Stage 4 adds the rule, integration, data, and security detail needed for controlled delivery
- Stage 5 prepares the team and trackers for execution readiness
- this scenario intentionally stops before build, deployment, or acceptance evidence

## 5. Risks and change markers

- scope creep into travel or leave process redesign
- privacy drift if leave data is shown too broadly
- source mismatch if employee identity is not stable
- change to the source-of-truth model should trigger roadmap revision
