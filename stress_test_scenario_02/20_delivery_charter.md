# Delivery Charter - SSD Staff Itinerary Tracker

Status: working draft
Stage: Stage 5 - Delivery Mobilization
Reference baseline: [Project Charter](18_project_charter.md)

## 1. Charter identity

- Initiative name: SSD staff itinerary tracker
- Charter version and date: v0.1 / 2026-04-10
- Stage context: Stage 5
- Owner: Delivery Owner / Project Manager
- Preparing party: Delivery Owner / Project Manager

## 2. Mobilization intent and scope guardrails

This charter prepares the team to execute the authorized visibility-layer solution within the approved baseline.

Scope guardrails:

- do not expand into travel redesign
- do not expand into leave-management redesign
- do not create a second system of record
- do not start build or deployment work until readiness conditions are satisfied

## 3. Team operating expectations

- Delivery Owner owns stage discipline, decision visibility, and readiness control.
- Programmer / Technical SME owns technical feasibility questions and controlled implementation detail.
- HR Leave Records Owner owns leave-data interpretation and access boundaries.
- Committee Secretary owns itinerary-source authority and revision visibility.
- Angie sponsors the work and resolves business decisions that sit above the source owners.
- IT Service Owner owns live support assumptions and handoff readiness.

## 4. Communication methods and cadence

- status review: weekly during mobilization
- working session: as needed for source, access, or rule clarification
- escalation path: unresolved ownership or control issues go to Angie and the Delivery Owner
- decision updates: recorded in the Decision Record Log as they occur

## 5. Governance controls during execution

- change path: any scope change must be recorded and approved before adoption
- issue path: unresolved assumptions or blockers stay visible in the assumptions/issues register
- decision path: material decisions stay in the decision log
- acceptance-readiness path: the delivery owner confirms readiness before any execution start
- status reporting: report by deliverable status, evidence status, and decision needs, not by activity alone

## 6. Tracker and control setup

| Decision log | Delivery Owner | Record material decisions and conditions |
