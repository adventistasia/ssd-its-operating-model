# Stakeholder Map And Personas

Status: working draft
Purpose: make the stakeholders discovered during the simulation visible, with their interests, concerns, and influence.

## 1. Stakeholder map

| Persona | Role | Interests | Concerns | Influence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Jane | Requestor and administrative coordinator | Wants one quick view of staff availability that reduces follow-up and confusion | Fears a solution that is too heavy to use or too manual to stay current | Medium |
| Angie | Sponsor and office operations decision-maker | Wants a trustworthy, low-maintenance solution that improves coordination without creating extra admin load | Will not support a duplicate system of record or unclear accountability | High |
| Delivery Owner / Project Manager | Delivery control owner | Wants a governed path that is proportionate, traceable, and supportable | Fears scope drift, unclear owners, or hidden design decisions | High |
| Programmer / Technical SME | Technical solution lead | Wants a feasible solution with clear rules, stable inputs, and low maintenance burden | Fears a quick build that silently creates a second record source | Medium-high |
| Committee Secretary | Formal itinerary approval owner | Wants itinerary status to remain authoritative and revision history to stay visible | Fears unofficial edits or status summaries that override the approved record | High |
| HR Leave Records Owner | Leave data owner | Wants leave status to remain governed by HR controls and privacy boundaries | Fears broad visibility, duplicate encoding, or incorrect reuse of leave data | High |
| Data Steward / Records Custodian | Governance and traceability owner | Wants a clear record trail, retention discipline, and no confusion over ownership | Fears a combined view that hides who owns the underlying record | Medium-high |
| IT Service Owner / Support Lead | Live service support owner | Wants the solution to be supportable after handoff with clear boundaries | Fears an unsupported tool that depends on one person or ad hoc fixes | Medium |

## 2. What each stakeholder wants from the work

- Jane wants speed and usefulness.
- Angie wants practical control without new bureaucracy.
- The Delivery Owner wants traceable governance and a right-sized artifact set.
- The Programmer wants rule clarity before any build is attempted.
- The Committee Secretary wants approved itinerary correctness protected.
- HR wants leave data kept under HR authority.
- The Data Steward wants traceability and retention discipline.
- IT support wants a maintainable service boundary.

## 3. Additional stakeholders discovered during the exercise

These stakeholders were not just background labels. They emerged as control-relevant participants once the framework forced ownership and acceptance questions into the open:

- Committee Secretary, because formal itinerary approval cannot be treated as informal status
- HR Leave Records Owner, because leave data is controlled by HR and cannot be duplicated casually
- Data Steward / Records Custodian, because the combined view needs a clear record and retention basis
- IT Service Owner / Support Lead, because a live tracker must be supportable after delivery

## 4. Influence notes

- Jane influences usability and adoption, but not formal control decisions.
- Angie influences sponsorship, prioritization, and authorization.
- The Committee Secretary and HR Leave Records Owner influence what can be treated as authoritative status.
- The Programmer influences feasibility and hidden complexity.
- The Data Steward and IT Service Owner influence whether the solution can be governed and supported after handoff.
