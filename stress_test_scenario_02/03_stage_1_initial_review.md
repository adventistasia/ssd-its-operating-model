## 1. Review identity

- Request title: SSD staff itinerary tracker
- Requestor / source: Jane, Administrative Assistant / Secretary
- Date identified: 2026-04-10
- Assessment Owner: Delivery Owner / Project Manager (simulated)

## 2. Statement of need

Office staff do not have one reliable place to check whether a staff member is:

- on an approved itinerary
- on leave or vacation
- expected to be in the office

The issue surfaced because staff repeatedly interrupt one another to confirm availability, meeting planning is delayed, and secretarial staff spend time reconstructing status from chats, email, and informal updates.

## 3. Desired outcome

Office support staff should be able to check one trusted view and quickly determine whether a staff member is away on approved itinerary, away on leave, or expected to be in the office, using the latest valid record.

## 4. Current challenges or friction points

- itinerary approvals are formal, but not easily visible in one place for office staff
- leave information is owned by HR and must stay controlled
- revisions to travel plans are not consistently visible
- office staff cannot tell which record is the latest approved one
- duplicate encoding would create a second source of truth
- there is no named owner yet for the combined visibility problem

## 5. Who is affected and why it matters

Primary affected groups:

- secretarial and administrative staff
- travelers
- staff waiting for follow-up or approvals
- HR leave records owner
- committee / itinerary approval support staff
- officials trying to schedule meetings

Why it matters:

- avoidable delays
- repeated follow-up
- poor meeting coordination
- risk of acting on outdated or unofficial itinerary information

## 6. Cost of inaction

Impact level: Moderate

Rationale:

- the issue does not appear mission-critical on day one
- however, it creates persistent operational waste, planning delays, and record confusion
- if it remains unresolved, staff will continue to maintain informal parallel views with low trust

## 7. Strategic and organizational fit

Fit: Broadly aligned

Rationale:

- the request fits the repository's control intent around visibility, explicit ownership, and reduction of key-person dependency
- the request is operationally useful and addresses recurring coordination waste
- sponsorship is not yet explicit, which weakens the current governance basis

## 8. Obvious constraints or dealbreakers

No immediate dealbreaker was found, but the following are visible constraints:

- sponsor is unclear at intake
- Outcome Owner is unclear
- Acceptance Authority is unclear
- source of truth for combined availability status is unclear
- leave records sit outside the itinerary-recording function
- revision handling must be visible rather than implied
