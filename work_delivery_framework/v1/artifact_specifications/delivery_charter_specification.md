# Delivery Charter Specification

## 1. Purpose

The `Delivery Charter` defines the external delivery engagement model, delivery responsibilities, working agreements, and handoff expectations needed for usable collaboration with or handoff to an external delivery team.

## 2. When Required

Required when the external engagement mode is either:

1. `Co-delivery`
2. `Full external delivery`

## 3. Required By

Gate 4 by default.

It may be required by Gate 3 when early delivery-team commitment is needed.

## 4. Required Contents

1. external engagement mode
2. delivery engagement model and roles or responsibilities
3. delivery cadence and working agreements
4. communication and escalation paths
5. high-level handoff expectations and acceptance workflow
6. access and environment expectations where applicable
7. decision and dependency interfaces between internal and external teams
8. internal named owners for framework gates and acceptance

## 5. Completeness Rules

The charter is complete when:

1. roles, interfaces, and responsibilities are explicit enough to avoid governance gaps
2. external parties can understand how work, escalation, decisions, and acceptance operate
3. for full external delivery, the approved package is close to standalone

## 6. Common Failure Conditions

1. the delivery mode is too vague to determine responsibilities
2. unowned escalation or coordination gaps remain
3. a full external delivery engagement still depends on recurring clarification of foundational business intent
