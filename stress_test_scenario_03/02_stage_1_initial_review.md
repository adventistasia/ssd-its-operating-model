## 2. Statement of need

Office staff do not have one reliable place to check whether a staff member is on an approved itinerary, on leave or vacation, or expected to be in the office.

The issue surfaced because itinerary revisions are frequent, leave records live in a separate source, and staff are currently reconciling status from chats, email, and informal follow-up.

## 3. Desired outcome

Office support staff should be able to check one trusted view and quickly determine whether a staff member is away on approved itinerary, away on leave, or expected to be in the office, using the latest approved or authoritative record.

## 4. Current challenges or friction points

- itinerary information is fragmented
- leave information is held in a separate control path
- staff availability is being inferred from informal communications
- revisions to travel plans are not consistently visible
- office staff cannot tell which record is latest or controlling
- status precedence is not defined
- there is no named owner yet for the combined visibility problem

## 5. Who is affected and why it matters

Primary affected groups:

- secretarial and administrative staff
- travelers
- office operations staff
- managers trying to schedule meetings
- committee secretariat staff
- HR leave record owners
- finance staff supporting travel-related processing
- internal audit or records management reviewers

Why it matters:

- avoidable delays
- repeated follow-up
- poor meeting coordination
- risk of acting on outdated or unofficial itinerary information
- audit and traceability gaps if status is changed without a visible basis

## 6. Cost of inaction

Impact level: High

Rationale:

- the issue is not life-critical, but it is persistent
- it will continue to create daily coordination waste
- it will continue to generate status disputes when itinerary revisions happen
- it will remain hard to defend during audit or records review

## 7. Strategic and organizational fit

Fit: Broadly aligned

Rationale:

- the request fits the repository's control intent around visibility, explicit ownership, and traceability
- the request is operationally useful and addresses recurring coordination waste
- sponsor willingness exists in principle, but only if governance and ownership are made explicit
