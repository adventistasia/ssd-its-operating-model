# Additional Stakeholders Discovered

Status: working draft  
Purpose: record the stakeholders that emerged during the simulation so they are not hidden off-screen.

## 1. Stakeholder summary

| Persona / stakeholder | Role | Interests | Concerns | Influence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Angie | Sponsor and budget holder | Wants a useful tool that justifies spend and reduces office friction | Will not support a solution that lacks governance or ownership | High |
| Committee Secretary | Owner of the approved itinerary record | Wants itinerary truth preserved and revisions traceable | Fears unofficial edits and false current-state display | High |
| HR Leave Records Owner | Steward of leave status | Wants leave handled from the correct source with proper privacy | Fears a merged view will become an uncontrolled proxy system of record | High |
| Office Operations Lead | Likely Outcome Owner | Wants fewer coordination delays and a dependable office view | Fears an app that is accurate but hard to use | High |
| Internal Audit / Records Management reviewer | Control reviewer | Wants evidence, history, and defensible retention | Fears silent overwrite or undocumented overrides | Medium to High |
| Security / Privacy reviewer | Control reviewer | Wants minimum necessary visibility and clean access rules | Fears overexposure of personal status data | Medium to High |
| IT Operations / Service Owner | Operational owner | Wants a supportable, documented service | Fears the solution becomes a one-person dependency | High |
| Finance or travel processing coordinator | Supporting stakeholder | Wants status visibility so follow-up and processing are not delayed | Fears duplicate data entry and unclear ownership | Medium |

## 2. Why these stakeholders emerged

These stakeholders were not all visible at intake. They emerged because the request touched multiple authoritative sources, revision history, role-based update rules, privacy and access concerns, auditability, and supportability beyond the initial builder.

## 3. Practical implications

- the app is not just an office convenience
- source ownership must be explicit
- acceptance may need to be split across usefulness, correctness, privacy, and support
- the Delivery Owner cannot leave these roles implied
- each of these stakeholders should be visible in later governance artifacts
