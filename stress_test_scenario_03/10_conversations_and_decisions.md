# Conversations And Decisions

Status: working draft  
Purpose: preserve the simulated multi-agent interactions that shaped the Delivery Owner's path.

## 1. Delivery Owner and Jane

Jane: visibility is the problem. Right now I have to ask around every time someone needs to know where staff are.  
Delivery Owner: what makes the current way unreliable?  
Jane: the answer changes depending on which message people remember. Sometimes the itinerary changed, sometimes leave changed, and nobody knows which one should win.

Delivery Owner: what would good look like?  
Jane: one screen, one answer, and no guessing.

Interpretation: Jane validated the need but did not solve the control gaps.

## 2. Delivery Owner and Angie

Delivery Owner: if this turns into a controlled app rather than a simple tracker, are you still willing to back it?  
Angie: yes, if it gives us a clean answer and if ownership is clear.

Delivery Owner: what would make you stop supporting it?  
Angie: if it becomes a free-for-all spreadsheet or if nobody can explain who owns the truth.

Interpretation: Angie made the custom app path possible, but only if the work stayed governed.

## 3. Delivery Owner and Programmer

Delivery Owner: can we just build a simple tracker and sort the details later?  
Programmer: not safely. If we do that, later means users already trust a broken rule.

Delivery Owner: what do you need before you can design this properly?  
Programmer: which source wins, who can change what, how conflicts display, and who signs off the audit trail.

Interpretation: the programmer converted vague friction into concrete definition needs.

## 4. Delivery Owner and Committee Secretary

Committee Secretary: if it is not in the approved record, it is not approved.  
Delivery Owner: what about the version people saw yesterday?  
Committee Secretary: keep it in history, but do not show it as current.

Delivery Owner: if the itinerary changes again, what should the app do?  
Committee Secretary: show the latest approved version and keep the previous one traceable.

Interpretation: itinerary authority is clear, but the app becomes more governed.

## 5. Delivery Owner and HR Leave Records Owner

Delivery Owner: can we merge leave and itinerary into one status column?  
HR Leave Records Owner: only if the rule is explicit and only if we do not expose more leave data than staff need.

Delivery Owner: what is your biggest concern?  
HR Leave Records Owner: people will treat the combined view as a replacement for the leave record. It is not.

Interpretation: leave status is a separate authority that must be respected.

## 6. Additional stakeholder introduced by the Delivery Owner

Role added: Internal Audit / Records Management reviewer.

Reason:

- the request now includes history, precedence, and traceability concerns
- the app's credibility depends on whether changes can be audited and retained
- nobody in the first round owned the evidence question

## 7. Decisions shaped by the conversations

- start at Stage 1 because the current stage was unclear
- use the full initiative path because the work crossed beyond light governance
- choose a custom app direction because a custom app can enforce role-based updates and precedence logic better than a weak tracker
- stop short of actual build because the exercise is about framework discipline, not implementation completion
