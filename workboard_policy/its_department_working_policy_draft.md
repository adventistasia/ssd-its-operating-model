# ITS Department Working Policy - Draft

## 1. Purpose

The ITS Department Working Policy provides simple and practical guidance on how the department operates and works together.

## 2. Policy Alignment and Authority

This Working Policy does not supersede, replace, or override the SSD Working Policy and the SSD Employee Handbook. In cases where there is any conflict or inconsistency, the SSD Working Policy and the SSD Employee Handbook shall prevail.

This Working Policy shall remain aligned with the ITS Operating Model. All departmental practices, ways of working, and internal guidelines should support and reflect the principles of the ITS Operating Model.

## 3. Scope

This policy applies to all ITS Department personnel and should be observed in all department-related work and activities.

## 4. Department Workboard

The ITS Department shall maintain a workboard to support visibility, coordination, and tracking of work.

### 4.1 Workboard Formats

This workboard may exist in both physical and digital formats.

- The physical workboard may be used for on-site collaboration and quick visual reference.
- The digital workboard shall serve as the primary and accessible source of truth for tracking tasks, progress, and priorities.

### 4.2 Work Documentation

- All planned work is expected to be documented in the workboard.
- A workbrief shall be created for each planned work.
- All work shall have a defined due date.
- All work and deliverables shall have an associated RAID (Risks, Assumptions, Issues, Dependencies) log.
- RAID log entries shall be recorded and closed/resolved as part of the delivery and acceptance of the work and its deliverables.

### 4.3 Work Roles

For each planned work, the following roles shall be defined and documented in the workbrief.

#### 4.3.1 Delivery Owner

**Primary Goal**

To ensure that the planned deliverables are completed and made ready for acceptance review in accordance with the authorized work.

**Accountability**

1. Delivery of the overall work.
2. Delivery of all deliverables within the work.

**Authority**

1. Declare delivery complete.
2. Declare deliverables ready for acceptance review.

#### 4.3.2 Decision Owner

**Primary Goal**

To ensure that work proceeds only when it is properly authorized, appropriately prioritized, and supported by approved resource commitments.

**Accountability**

1. Authorization of the work.
2. Approval of the workbrief prior to execution.
3. Decisions affecting scope and priority during execution.
4. Approval of resource allocation.

**Authority**

1. Approve the workbrief.
2. Authorize scope changes during execution.
3. Authorize priority changes during execution.
4. Approve resource allocation.
5. Exercise final authority in cases of disagreement regarding acceptance of work.

#### 4.3.3 Acceptance Owner

**Primary Goal**

To ensure that deliverables and work are evaluated against clear acceptance and closure criteria before they are accepted and closed.

**Accountability**

1. Definition of clear acceptance criteria.
2. Conduct of quality assessment.
3. Validation that delivered work meets the defined acceptance criteria.
4. Assurance that deliverables under this role have well-defined, clear, and appropriate acceptance criteria before the work leaves the definition stage.

**Authority**

1. Declare acceptance review complete.
2. Declare closure review complete.

The following conditions apply:

- One individual may hold multiple roles.
- It is recommended, but not required, that the Delivery Owner and Acceptance Owner are different individuals.
- In cases of disagreement regarding acceptance of work, the Decision Owner shall have final authority.

### 4.4 Workboard Management

A Workboard Manager shall be assigned to maintain the workboard.

### 4.5 Workboard Manager Responsibilities

The Workboard Manager shall be responsible for:

- ensuring the quality of work briefs on the workboard;
- ensuring compliance with agreed conventions;
- ensuring that the board meeting schedule, reporting cycle, and recurring activities are followed consistently and on time;
- following the documented process;
- ensuring that required evidence, as specified in the acceptance criteria and closure criteria, is present on the workboard; however, determining whether the evidence is sufficient for acceptance remains the responsibility of the Acceptance Owner;
- triggering the start of acceptance review of deliverables; and
- clearing completed items from the workboard.

### 4.6 Team Responsibilities

- All team members are expected to keep the workboard updated to ensure transparency and alignment across the department.

### 4.7 Board Operating Cadence

#### 4.7.1 Workboard Review

- The team shall meet on a regular schedule to conduct a Workboard Review.
- The schedule for the Workboard Review shall be determined by the team.
- If a scheduled Workboard Review falls on a non-working day, it shall be deferred to the next regular review session, unless the team agrees to hold a special session.
- Items must exist on the physical workboard to be eligible for review.

#### 4.7.2 Board Walk

- The team shall conduct a Board Walk on a daily basis.
- The purpose of the Board Walk is to verify flow and correctness of work state, not to provide status updates.
- If a work item is in the wrong column, blocked, or unclear:
  - it shall be corrected immediately; or
  - a follow-up discussion or meeting shall be scheduled.
- The focus of the Board Walk shall be on identifying what is preventing work from progressing, rather than reporting individual activity.

### 4.8 Work Acceptance and Closure

- The Workboard Manager shall trigger the start of acceptance review of deliverables after the Delivery Owner has declared that delivery is complete.
- The Acceptance Owner shall declare when acceptance review is complete.
- If deliverables are accepted, the work may proceed to closure review.
- If deliverables are not accepted in full, the Workboard Manager shall organize a meeting to resolve the non-acceptance of deliverables.
- The Acceptance Owner shall conduct the closure review for work that proceeds to closure review.
- The Workboard Manager shall trigger the formal closure of the work after the closure review confirms that the work meets the closure criteria.
- The Workboard Manager shall clear closed items from the workboard.

## 5. Workboard Metrics

The ITS Department shall define and monitor metrics to ensure the effectiveness and discipline of workboard usage.

### 5.1 Metric Definitions

- **Work Brief Redefinition** – The number of times a work brief is changed after it has been authorized for delivery.

## 6. Review and Updates

This Working Policy may be reviewed and updated as needed to ensure continued alignment with the SSD Working Policy, the SSD Employee Handbook, and the ITS Operating Model.
